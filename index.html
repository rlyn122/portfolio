Cultural Analytics: Cuisines and Stereotypes

The purpose of the cultural analysis is to investigate how a cuisine influences the language of reviews written by its consumers. We hypothesize that racial stereotypes will be reflected in both the positive and negative reviews. For example, we believe that Chinese restaurants have more negative comments about staff and cleanliness, as well as more positive comments about the cost of the food. We are using Topic Modeling, Top Adjectives, and TFIDF Scores Analyses to do research.

Research: Location-Based Collaborative Filtering

The goal of this research is to identify possible areas of optimization for location recommendation problems through collaborative filtering. Whether it is next location recommendation, mobility prediction, outlier detection, or another problem, we search to apply collaborative filtering (similar to that used for the Netflix Challenge) to a location context. Using the Gowalla dataset from Stanford SNAP, with professor Andreas Zufle at Emory University, I am exploring collaborative filtering models through tensor and matrix factorizations.
